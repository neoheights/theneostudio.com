Modern Kitchen Layouts That
Enhance Workflow

The Neo Studio

Kitchen design is fundamentally a problem of logistics. The most beautiful kitchen in the world fails if it's frustrating to cook in. At The Neo Studio, we design kitchens around how people actually use them — and the results are transformative.

The Five Classic Layouts

Every kitchen is a variation of five layouts. Understanding which suits your space and cooking style is the foundation of a great kitchen design:

  • Straight (Single Wall): Best for very small spaces. All elements on one wall. Limited counter space but efficient for one cook.
  • Galley (Parallel Walls): Two parallel counters facing each other. Supremely efficient for a single cook. The layout of choice in professional kitchens.
  • L-Shaped: Most versatile for Indian homes. Natural work triangle, allows dining area in the same space.
  • U-Shaped: Maximum storage and counter space. Ideal for serious cooks. Requires a larger room.
  • Island: Adds prep space, storage, seating, and a social focal point. Requires generous clearance on all sides.
Layout Guide

For most Indian 2 and 3 BHK apartments, the L-shaped kitchen is the optimal choice. It balances efficiency, storage, and the ability to incorporate a dining area in the same space.

The L-Shaped Kitchen — Most Versatile

For most Indian homes, the L-shaped kitchen is the sweet spot. It provides a natural work triangle, allows for a dining area in the same space, and scales well from compact to generous room sizes.

We design L-shaped kitchens with the hob on one arm and the sink on the other, with the refrigerator at the corner entry point. This creates a natural flow: retrieve from fridge → prep at counter → cook at hob → clean at sink.

A kitchen that works well doesn't feel like work. The best layouts make cooking feel intuitive — everything is exactly where you expect it to be.

The Island — When Space Allows

A kitchen island adds prep space, storage, seating, and a social focal point in one element. The minimum clearance around an island is 90cm on all sides — less than this and the kitchen becomes difficult to navigate with multiple people.

When space allows, an island is the single upgrade that most transforms a kitchen's functionality and social character. It becomes the natural gathering point for family and guests.

Workflow Zones

We divide every kitchen into five zones, and organise storage around them:

  1. Consumables Zone: Pantry, refrigerator — near the kitchen entry
  2. Non-Consumables Zone: Crockery, utensils, cookware — near the cooking zone
  3. Cleaning Zone: Sink, dishwasher — with counter space on both sides
  4. Preparation Zone: Maximum counter space, near the sink
  5. Cooking Zone: Hob, oven — with heat-resistant counter on both sides

The Importance of Counter Height

Standard counter height is 85–90cm. But the right height depends on the primary cook's height. A counter that's too low causes back strain; too high causes shoulder fatigue.

For custom kitchens, we always verify the ideal counter height for the household. The formula: elbow height minus 15cm = ideal counter height. This small adjustment makes a significant difference in cooking comfort over years of use.

Corner Solutions

Corners are the most wasted space in any kitchen. Modern solutions that make corners work:

  • Carousel units: Rotating shelves that bring items to you
  • Le Mans units: Pull-out shelves that swing out of the corner
  • Magic corner units: Two-tier pull-out systems that maximise every centimetre
  • Diagonal corner sink: Places the sink at the corner, freeing up straight runs for prep
