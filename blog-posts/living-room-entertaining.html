Creating the Perfect Living Room
for Entertaining Guests

The Neo Studio

The living room is where first impressions are made and memories are created. When you entertain guests, this space carries the full weight of your hospitality. Here's how The Neo Studio designs living rooms that are as welcoming as they are beautiful.

Define Your Seating Arrangement First

The biggest mistake in living room design is choosing furniture before defining the seating arrangement. Start with the conversation: how many people do you typically host? Where is the focal point — a TV, a fireplace, a view?

Arrange seating to face that focal point and face each other, not the walls. Conversation is the purpose of a living room — the layout should make it effortless.

Layout Rule

Leave at least 45cm between the sofa and coffee table, and at least 90cm for main walkways. Tight spaces make guests feel uncomfortable even if they can't articulate why.

The Sofa is the Anchor

Invest in a quality sofa. It's the piece that defines the room's character and takes the most use. For entertaining, an L-shaped sectional or a sofa-plus-two-chairs arrangement works best.

Choose a fabric that's durable and easy to clean — performance fabrics and leather are both excellent choices for a home that hosts regularly. Avoid light-coloured fabrics unless you're committed to professional cleaning.

The best living rooms for entertaining feel generous — generous with space, with light, with comfort. Guests should feel immediately at ease.

Lighting Sets the Mood

Overhead lighting alone kills the atmosphere of any gathering. Layer your lighting for maximum flexibility:

  • Ambient: Dimmable overhead light or a statement chandelier
  • Accent: Floor lamp in the corner, table lamps on side tables
  • Decorative: LED strip behind the TV unit or along shelving

Dimmers on the main light give you full control over the mood — bright for family gatherings, warm and low for dinner parties.

Create Conversation Zones

In larger living rooms, create two distinct zones — a main seating area and a secondary lounge corner. This allows guests to naturally break into smaller conversations without the room feeling fragmented.

A pair of armchairs with a small side table in a corner creates an intimate nook that guests gravitate towards naturally. It also makes the room feel more curated and intentional.

The Coffee Table — Functional and Beautiful

A coffee table should be at the right height (same as sofa cushions), large enough to hold drinks for everyone, and styled with a few curated objects — a tray, a candle, a book. Avoid cluttering it.

For entertaining, a coffee table with a lower shelf for extra storage is ideal. Coasters, remotes, and small items can be stored out of sight but within reach.

Storage That Hides the Chaos

Entertainment requires stuff — remotes, chargers, board games, extra cushions. Built-in storage units or a well-designed TV unit with closed cabinets keeps the room looking effortlessly tidy even when it's in full use.

  • TV unit with closed lower cabinets for AV equipment and remotes
  • Ottoman with internal storage for throws and cushions
  • Built-in shelving with a mix of open display and closed storage
