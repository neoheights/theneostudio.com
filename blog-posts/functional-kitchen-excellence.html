The Culinary Studio:
Functional Kitchen Excellence

The Neo Studio

A great kitchen is the most used room in any home — and the most unforgiving of poor design. At The Neo Studio, we approach every kitchen project as a culinary studio: a space where beauty and function are inseparable.

The Work Triangle — Still Relevant

The classic kitchen work triangle (sink, stove, refrigerator) remains the foundation of efficient kitchen design. In modern kitchens, this evolves into work zones — prep zone, cooking zone, cleaning zone, and storage zone — each thoughtfully positioned to minimise unnecessary movement.

The total distance of the work triangle should ideally be between 4 and 8 metres. Too small and the kitchen feels cramped; too large and you're walking unnecessary distances while cooking.

Pro Tip

Place the refrigerator at the entry point of the kitchen so family members can access it without entering the cooking zone — this prevents traffic conflicts during meal prep.

Modular Kitchens Done Right

Modular kitchens offer flexibility and precision that site-built kitchens can't match. We design modular systems with premium carcasses, soft-close hinges, and finishes that range from matte lacquer to natural wood veneer.

Every cabinet is planned around your actual storage needs — not a generic template. We conduct a detailed inventory of your cookware, appliances, and pantry items before designing a single cabinet.

The best kitchen isn't the most beautiful one. It's the one that makes cooking feel effortless — and happens to look stunning too.

Countertop Choices That Last

The countertop takes the most abuse in any kitchen. Here's how the main options compare:

  • Engineered Quartz: Best all-rounder — durable, non-porous, low maintenance, wide range of finishes
  • Natural Granite: Unique character, very durable, requires annual sealing
  • Marble: Unmatched beauty, requires careful maintenance, best for low-use kitchens
  • Compact Laminate: Budget-friendly, decent durability, limited premium feel

Lighting in the Kitchen

Most kitchens are under-lit. A well-designed kitchen has three layers of lighting:

  1. Ambient lighting: Overhead recessed lights or a central pendant for general illumination
  2. Task lighting: Under-cabinet LED strips directly above prep areas — essential for safe chopping
  3. Accent lighting: Inside glass-fronted cabinets or above open shelving for atmosphere

Storage That Solves Real Problems

Pull-out drawers, corner carousel units, tall pantry cabinets, and integrated spice racks — smart storage is what separates a good kitchen from a great one. We design storage around how you actually cook, not how a catalogue suggests you should.

  • Deep drawers for pots and pans — far more accessible than base cabinets
  • Pull-out waste bins integrated into the cabinetry
  • Tall pantry units with internal pull-out shelves
  • Dedicated appliance garages for mixers, toasters, and coffee machines

Ventilation and Appliance Integration

A kitchen that looks beautiful but smells like last night's dinner has failed. Proper chimney sizing, placement, and ducting is non-negotiable. The chimney should be at least as wide as the hob, ideally wider.

We also integrate appliances — ovens, microwaves, dishwashers — seamlessly into the cabinetry for a clean, uncluttered look. Built-in appliances at the right height eliminate bending and improve ergonomics significantly.
